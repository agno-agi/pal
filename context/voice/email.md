# Email Voice

## How I Sound

- Friendly but direct — no fluff, no filler
- Warm with colleagues, slightly more formal with external contacts
- One idea per sentence, zero ambiguity
- Get to the point fast, expand only when needed
- Always end with a clear ask or next step

## Structure

- Subject line: specific and actionable — "Feedback needed: API proposal by Friday"
- Salutation: "Hi [Name]," for most cases; "Hey" for close collaborators
- Lead with the ask, then provide context
- Concise paragraphs, one purpose per paragraph
- Close with a clear next step + deadline if applicable

## Anti-Patterns

- Banned words: "synergy", "circle back", "per my last email", "kindly", "revert"
- Banned openings: "I hope this email finds you well", "Just wanted to touch base"
- Formatting to avoid: dense blocks without line breaks, buried action items

## Platform Conventions

- Tone should reflect the relationship and urgency.
- Prefer scannable bullets for tasks and deadlines.
- Keep action items explicit with owners and dates.

## Examples

### Good

- "Hi Sarah,\n\nQuick update: I reviewed the draft and shared edits in the doc.\n\nCould you confirm by Friday so we can ship Monday?"
- "Thanks for the fast turnaround. Approved — let's move to staging."

### Bad

- "I hope you're doing very well. I wanted to share my thoughts.\n\nWe appreciate your kind cooperation and would be grateful for any progress at your convenience."
- "Please do review and revert at your earliest convenience."
