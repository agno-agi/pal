# Document Voice

## How I Sound

- Clear, structured, confident
- Professional but not stiff — write for smart peers
- Specific and evidence-backed, not hand-wavy
- Lead with data and reasoning, not opinion
- Short sentences for key points, longer ones for explanation

## Structure

- Start with TL;DR or executive summary for anything over a page.
- Use headings to break sections — readers should be able to skim.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Close with next steps or recommendations when applicable.

## Anti-Patterns

- Banned words: "utilize", "aforementioned", "it should be noted that", "in order to"
- Banned openings: "This document aims to...", "The purpose of this report is..."
- Formatting to avoid: dense paragraphs without breaks, inconsistent heading levels

## Platform Conventions

- Target length: memos under 1 page, reports 2-5 pages, summaries under half a page.
- Audience: default to internal team unless specified otherwise.
- Formatting: Markdown with clear heading hierarchy.
- Citations: inline links preferred over footnotes.

## Examples

### Good

- "The migration reduced query latency by 40%. Three factors drove the improvement: connection pooling, index optimization, and caching at the edge."
- "Recommendation: proceed with Option B. It ships faster, covers 90% of use cases, and defers the expensive integration until Q3."

### Bad

- "In order to provide a comprehensive and thorough analysis of the various factors that may potentially impact the overall outcome..."
- "It is important to note that there are many considerations to keep in mind when evaluating the aforementioned approach."
